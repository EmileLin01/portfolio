Garchomp

HP: 108
Attack: 130
Defense: 95
Sp. Atk: 80
Sp. Def: 85
Speed: 102
Strengths

Garchomp is a ubiquitous Pokemon in the BW2 OU metagame owing to its fantastic typing, overall great stats, and versatile movesets.

Garchomp is most often used for its unmatched offensive prowess. With its unique dual Dragon/Ground STABs and a monstrous 130 Attack stat, Garchomp has the tools to decimate the majority of the metagame. Even defensive Pokemons that resist its Outrages and Earthquakes, like Skarmory and Bronzong, fall prey to its Fire Fangs. Supporting its superb offenses is its excellent Speed stat of 102, which is just fast enough to outspeed some of the most dangerous Pokemons in the tier, such as Excadrill, Thundurus-T, Volcanaro, Heatran, Kyurem-B, and +1 Dragonite/+1 Salamence. These traits make Garchomp an ideal sweeper that can either destroy defensive cores with a Life Orb + Sword Dance set or reliably clean up weakened teams with a Choice Scarf. Its respectable 80 Special Attack stat even allows it to drop Draco Meteors and launch Fire Blasts at unsuspecting physical walls like Gliscor and Ferrothorn, respectively.

Garchomp also shines defensively. Dragon/Ground is a strong defensive typing, being weak to only opposing Dragon moves and the occasional Ice move. This, in conjunction with Garchomp’s surprising bulk, makes it an effective Stealth Rock setter that can switch in on common threats such as Tyranitar, Heatran, Thundurus-T, and Jirachi.

Checks & Counters

Despite its numerous qualities, Garchomp struggles against faster threats, including Latios, Alakazam, Keldo, Terrakion, and Starmie. If Garchomp runs Choice Scarf, it will lack the firepower to get past physical walls like Ferrothorn, Skarmory, Landorus-T, and Gliscor. Finally, because it lacks a recovery move, it is vulnerable to chip damage and can be often picked out by priority users like Scizor, Dragonite, and Breloom.

Conclusion

While Garchomp can be configured to beat any Pokemon in the metagame, it is balanced by the fact that it can never beat all the Pokemons in the tier. Building Garchomp to counter one set of threats will always make it susceptible to another. Even so, a well-played Garchomp that synergizes with its team is as close to flawless as any Pokemon can be.

Lucario

HP: 70
Attack: 110
Defense: 70
Sp. Atk: 115
Sp. Def: 70
Speed: 90
Strengths

At first, Lucario may seem like a middling Pokemon in the DPP metagame: average speed, low bulk, a relatively useless offensive STAB in Steel, and slightly above-average offensive stats. This cannot be possibly further from the truth. Despite its Speed stat of 90 and its higher Special Attack, Lucario is easily one of the fastest and most powerful physical sweepers in the DPP OU metagame.

Thanks to STAB Close Combat working off 110 Attack, two excellent priority moves in Extreme Speed and Bullet Punch, useful coverage moves in Ice Punch and Crunch, one Sword Dance is all Lucario needs to start wreaking havoc on the opposing team. The sheer destructiveness of an Adamant +2 Lucario holding a Life Orb cannot be understated:

 Close Combat vs. 252/252+ Skarmory: 84.4% - 99.7%
 Close Combat vs. 252/0+ Skarmory: 101.5% - 119.5%
 Close Combat vs. 252/252+ Bronzong: 95.2% - 100%
 Close Combat vs. 252/176+ Hippowdon: 80.7% - 95%
 Crunch vs. 252/220+ Celebi: 81.2% - 95.5%
 Crunch vs. 252/252+ Rotom-A: 100% - 117.8%
 Extreme Speed vs. 0/0 Starmie: 97.3% - 114.6%
 Extreme Speed vs 0/0 Zapdos: 79.1% - 93.1%
 Bullet Punch vs. 0/0 Gengar: 95.8% - 113%
 Bullet Punch vs. 0/0 Tyranitar: 89.7% - 105.6%
Checks & Counters

Its astounding power notwithstanding, Lucario’s potential is limited by its imperfect coverage. If it runs Ice Punch, it loses to Rotom-A and Celebi. If it runs Crunch, it loses to Gliscor. In addition to its limited coverage, Lucario also falls to faster threats that it cannot K.O. with +2 Extreme Speed and Bullet Punch. These counters include Gyarados, Timid Gliscor, Timid Suicune, and Jirachi. Well-built teams will always have a Pokemon or two that Lucario struggles against; removing these threats with a teammate or sufficiently weakening them is key to Lucario’s success.

Conclusion

Because of these glaring weaknesses, Lucario works best as a late-game sweeper that should only take the field when its checks and counters have been eliminated or battered. With a single Sword Dance under its belt, Lucario blows the game wide open by K.O.ing walls with Close Combat and picking off faster Pokemons with Extreme Speed or Bullet Punch.

Suicune

HP: 100
Attack: 75
Defense: 115
Sp. Atk: 90
Sp. Def: 115
Speed: 85
Strengths

Whether acting as a supremely bulky win condition or as a fluent special sweeper, Suicune is a premier force in the DPP in the metagame.

Suicune is most notorious for its "Crocune" set, which aims to stonewall offensive threats while amping up its own offenses for a late-game sweep. Great 100/115/115 mixed defenses mean that Suicune rarely gets taken out in one turn, and its access to the Rest and Sleep Talk combo allows it to remain healthy without losing momentum. Calm Mind can then be used to boost its STAB-powered Surf while rendering it nearly invulnerable to special attacks. Suicune thrives at soaking attacks from the likes of Heatran, Infernape, Swampert, Gyarados, and Metagross and setting up in their face. After one or two Calm Minds, even Grass- and Electric-type Pokemons that rely on special attacks, such as certain variants of Celebi, Roserade, Zapdos, Rotom-A, and Jolteon can be overcome. Suicune’s abilities to remain healthy throughout the match and to simultaneously increase its offenses and defensives makes it an extremely alluring Pokemon to build around.

Suicune is also well-suited to take on a more offensive role. While its Special Attack stat of 90 may seem unremarkable, Suicune’s natural bulk affords it plenty of opportunities to use Calm Mind to boost its power to threatening levels. This, coupled with decent speed and strong coverage in Ice Beam and HP Electric, allows it to beat Pokemons that its bulkier counterpart would struggle against, including Breloom, Latias, Dragonite, Empoleon, and Gyarados. Suicune can thus serve as bait, luring in the aforementioned Pokemons with Calm Mind, cleanly K.O.ing them with a boosted Ice Beam or HP Electric, and paving the way for another sweeper to take over the match. Offensive Suicune offers a rare blend of bulk and power, and it will usually take down one or two opponents before fainting.

Checks & Counters

Suicune is not without its flaws. Mono-attacking sets lose to physical threats that resist Surf, such as Dragonite, Kingdra, Abomasnow, Breloom, and Gyarados. Pokemons packing utility moves such as Encore, Trick, Taunt, and Roar/Whirlwind also completely ruin these sets. Vaporeon and Quagsire are particularly good candidates to run these moves thanks to their immunity to Water moves.

Offensive Suicune lacks the raw power to muscle through special walls that it cannot hit super effectively, including Blissey, Snorlax, and Cresselia. Faster threats wielding Electric and Grass moves, namely Raikou, Zapdos, Jolteon, Roserade, and Celebi can be problematic as well. Even without dedicated answers, offensive Suicune can be easily taken down via a combination of entry hazards, status ailments, and chip damage.

Conclusion

Sporting well-rounded stats and the right moves to exploit them, Suicune adeptly suits for two crucial roles in the tier: a mixed wall that doubles as a late-game sweeper and a mid-game threat that can remove key threats for its teammates. Play Suicune to its strengths, and it will be sure to reward you with its consistency.
